    Risk Perception as a Determinant of Pro-Environmental Behaviours:
    A Study of Public Responses to COVID-19

    Risk perception related to environmental issues such as the onset of a pandemic may be a determinant of pro-environmental behaviours. Because of the current unprecedented COVID-19 pandemic, people have become extremely susceptible to the adverse impacts of disease infection on psychological well-being. A survey (n=232) was conducted focusing on public perception of the COVID-19 pandemic using four measures with an attempt to determine the possible link between perceived risk and maladaptive behavioural responses. The analysis found a significant effect of perceived risk on the maladaptive behavioural responses of the participants during the pandemic.

    Effect of Air Pollution on Human Cognitive Performance:
    An Empirical Investigation

    Air Pollution in relation with cognitive disorders are rooted in several interplaying factors within the environment leading to neurodegenerative processes. Reduction in worker productivity, traffic accidents, depression, etc may all be the outcome of decline in cognitive performance. The consequences of exposure to air pollutants stands vastly understated to respiratory problems or to immediate health consequences, mostly unnoticing its effects on neurodegerenation. It can be said that improvement in air quality may yield tremendous benefits in terms of welfare.
